You Know Your City — But Do You Own It?

Urban explorers know something most people miss: every city has hidden layers. Back alleys with forgotten murals. Courtyards that never appear on any map. Rooftop vistas that belong to whoever finds them first.

But there's no record of your discoveries. No proof you were there. No game that keeps score across the whole city over months and years.

Google Maps shows everyone the same streets. Social media posts vanish after a day. There's nowhere to plant your flag — until now.

MapRaiders: Urban Exploration Game Built on Real GPS

MapRaiders is a GPS territory MMO where the entire city — every block, park, plaza, and side street — is the game world. Your physical movement is the controller.

🗺️

Territory Claiming

Every GPS coordinate you physically visit can become territory you own. Walk a block, claim a block. The map reflects your real-world presence.

🎙️

Audio Echoes

Drop voice recordings anchored to exact locations. Other explorers hear your echo when they physically reach that spot — like invisible graffiti with sound.

🏚️

White Zones

Unclaimed areas appear as white space on the MapRaiders map. For urban explorers, white zones are not gaps — they're invitations. First there, first owned.

⚡

Decay System

Territory weakens if you stop visiting. This isn't a bug — it's designed to keep the game alive. A returning explorer reclaims. An absent one loses ground.

How MapRaiders Works for Urban Explorers

  • 1
    Head out and walk Open MapRaiders and start moving. Every GPS coordinate you physically visit becomes territory you can claim. No fixed waypoints, no spawns — just your movement.
  • 2
    Claim, stamp, and echo Mark zones as your territory. Drop audio echoes at spots that matter — a hidden arch, a quiet corner, a view nobody talks about. Your city, your story.
  • 3
    Defend, expand, compete Other players can contest your zones. Explore more to defend. Form a crew to hold larger territory. The urban map is never final.

Who This Is For

MapRaiders is built for people who already love the city and want more from it:

  • Urban explorers who seek out hidden architecture, abandoned spaces, and forgotten corners.
  • Daily commuters who take different routes just to see what's there — now that habit has a game layer.
  • Local history buffs who want to leave knowledge anchored to the places it belongs.
  • Competitive walkers who want a reason to explore new neighborhoods instead of the same loop.
  • Crews and friend groups who want to hold territory together as an organized clan.

If you've ever thought "I know this city better than anyone" — MapRaiders is where you prove it.
