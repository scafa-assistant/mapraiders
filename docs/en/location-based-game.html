The Problem

What's Missing from Today's Location-Based Games?

Location-based games haven't evolved since Pokémon GO. The genre promised a revolution — your physical world as a game. But after a decade, the pattern is the same: collect, tap, repeat. GPS is decoration, not the core mechanic. The map is there, but it doesn't belong to you.

What's missing is real ownership, real strategy, and real community. You shouldn't be collecting points on a map — you should be owning territory. Not playing alone — but as part of a community fighting for the same streets you walk. MapRaiders is built on exactly this premise. Your city. Your empire. Your rules.

FAQ

Quick Answers

What is a location-based game?
A location-based game uses your real GPS position as the core mechanic. You move in the physical world, and those movements have direct consequences in the game. MapRaiders takes this further: you claim persistent territory, defend it with interactive mini-games, and compete against real neighbors for control of real streets.
How is MapRaiders different from Pokémon GO?
MapRaiders focuses on persistent territory ownership rather than temporary gym control, active interactive defense games rather than automated combat, player-generated content (quests, Echos, challenges), and zero pay-to-win. It's a strategy MMO built on GPS — not a collection game.
Does MapRaiders work in any city?
Yes. MapRaiders uses OpenStreetMap data and works worldwide in any city, town, or village. The more players active in your area, the more competitive and dynamic the map becomes. Early players claim the best territories.
Do I need to walk a lot to succeed?
Movement matters, but so does strategy. Smart defense setups, strong alliances, and tactical territory management can compensate for less walking time. A clever player with a smaller territory often beats a passive player with a large one.
Is MapRaiders free?
Yes, completely free. No pay-to-win, no premium items that affect gameplay, no battle pass. All players compete on equal footing from day one.
