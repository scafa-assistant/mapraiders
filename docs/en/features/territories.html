How it works

Four steps to conquer your city

01

Walk

Start territory tracking in the app and head outside. Every meter you cover is recorded as a GPS point on the map.

02

Close the route

Return to your starting point to close the loop. The app detects the closed polygon automatically — no manual input needed.

03

Claim the land

Your territory appears on the shared map in your chosen color. Everyone in the game can see it — it's yours now.

04

Defend it

Place defense games on your territory. Attackers must win a mini-game to take your land. Win the game, keep the ground.

FAQ

Common questions

What is territory claiming in MapRaiders?
Territory claiming is the core mechanic of MapRaiders. You physically walk, run or cycle a route in the real world. When you close the route by returning to your starting point, the enclosed area becomes your territory and appears in your chosen color on the map.
How do I claim territory?
Open MapRaiders and start territory tracking. Walk any route outdoors — every GPS point is recorded. When you return to your starting location, the polygon closes automatically and the area is claimed as your territory.
What happens if I don't defend my territory?
MapRaiders has a decay system. Territories that are not visited regularly will slowly shrink over time. To keep your territory at full size, you need to walk through it periodically.
Can other players take my territory?
Yes. Other players can challenge your territory by physically walking to it and initiating a defense game. If they win the mini-game, they claim the territory. You can protect your land by placing defense games on it.
How large can a territory get?
Territories grow as large as the route you walk. There is no hard size limit, but larger territories require more effort to defend and maintain against decay. Adjacent territories you own are automatically merged into larger zones.
What are territory multipliers?
Territory multipliers boost the points and influence you gain from owning land. They can be earned through quests, events, and special challenges. High-traffic or strategically located territories often carry natural bonuses.
