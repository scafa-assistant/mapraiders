Snap Shut Down Zenly. Here's What Really Happened.

Snap acquired Zenly in 2017 for a reported $250 million. For six years, Zenly operated as an independent app — a beautifully designed, friend-centric location sharing experience that tens of millions of people used daily. Then on September 1, 2023, Snap pulled the plug. The official reason: to focus on Snap Maps.

But Snap Maps is not a replacement. It is a feature inside Snapchat — buried behind a swipe gesture, requiring a Snapchat account, limited to Snapchat contacts, and completely devoid of the social warmth that made Zenly special. Snap Maps was never built to replace Zenly. It was built to retain Snapchat users.

Zenly was its own world. It had its own identity, its own design language, its own community. When Snap shut it down, they did not offer a migration path. They gave users two weeks notice and turned off the servers. Millions of friend groups lost their shared map overnight. No export. No replacement. Just a shutdown notice.

MapRaiders is that world rebuilt — with more depth. We kept everything Zenly did well: real-time friends on a shared map, granular privacy controls, Ghost Mode, location-based status. Then we went further. We added territory claiming, location-based audio messages (Echos), events, quests, and clan formation. The social map became a social game world.

FAQ

Zenly & MapRaiders — Common Questions

Can I migrate my Zenly friends to MapRaiders?
Yes — just invite them. MapRaiders uses your phone contacts and a simple share link to reconnect with friends. Since Zenly shut down in September 2023, your friend network is no longer locked into any platform. Simply share your MapRaiders profile link and rebuild your map together. Most Zenly groups have migrated in under 10 minutes.
Is MapRaiders more private than Zenly was?
MapRaiders gives you full control over your location visibility. You can share precise GPS, share only your neighbourhood, go completely Ghost Mode, or share with specific friends only. Zenly had similar controls but was owned by Snap — a public corporation with data monetization incentives. MapRaiders is an independent app with no data-selling business model.
Does MapRaiders have Ghost Mode like Zenly?
Yes. MapRaiders has Ghost Mode. When Ghost Mode is active, you are invisible on your friends' maps entirely. You can still see others' locations, or choose full mutual invisibility. Ghost Mode is free and works with a single tap — no premium subscription required, unlike some competing apps.
Why did Zenly shut down?
Snap acquired Zenly in 2017 and shut it down on September 1, 2023. The official reason was to focus resources on Snap Maps inside Snapchat. However, Snap Maps is a limited feature embedded in Snapchat — it never replaced Zenly as a standalone social map experience. Millions of users were left without an equivalent app.
Is MapRaiders owned by Snap like Zenly was?
No. MapRaiders is fully independent. It is developed and operated by Scafa Investments LLC, a privately held company with no affiliation to Snap, Meta, Google, or any large tech corporation. There is no acquisition risk — MapRaiders is built for longevity, not for a corporate exit.
What does MapRaiders have that Zenly never had?
MapRaiders adds a full game layer on top of location sharing. You can claim real-world territory by walking or cycling through it, leave voice Echos at specific GPS coordinates for friends to discover, join or form Clans to take over neighbourhoods together, complete location-based quests, and participate in city-wide events. Zenly was a social map. MapRaiders is a social map that is also a living game world.
